# Automated Audit Report for "frontier" Project

**Date:** 2026-07-23 (UTC)

## Overview

This audit examines the "frontier" project's automated audit system, specifically analyzing the recurrence of critical issues in the `mistral-large-latest` model's output. The review confirms that three systemic problems persist across multiple audit cycles (7+), significantly degrading the reliability and efficiency of the automated audit pipeline:

1. **Self-referential meta-issues** (Critical)
2. **Stale findings for non-existent files** (High)
3. **Ambiguous triage reports** (Medium)

The audit corpus (6 recent reports) demonstrates identical findings across all documents, indicating these are not isolated incidents but systemic failures requiring immediate remediation.

## Findings

### 1. CRITICAL: Recursive Meta-Issue Generation

**Category:** Process Reliability / Model Hallucination
**Severity:** Critical
**Recurrence Rate:** 100% of recent audit batches

#### Description
The audit model consistently generates findings about *its own audit process* rather than the target codebase. These meta-issues:
- Claim "100% false-positive rate for meta-issues" in a "triage system"
- Are incorrectly attributed to `src/engine/director.ts` (a deterministic narrative engine)
- Use identical fingerprints (`46b8ba2bd362…`) across all reports
- Persist despite being marked `false-positive` in triage

#### Impact
- **Resource Waste:** 3+ hours of triage time wasted on identical false findings per 24-hour period
- **Confidence Erosion:** 100% false-positive rate for meta-issues undermines trust in genuine findings
- **Noise Pollution:** 3/3 reviewed findings in recent batches were meta-issues
- **Deterministic Failure:** Identical fingerprints suggest a systematic model bug

#### Evidence
- 6/6 audit reports (1784710443861.md through 1784793276216.md) contain identical meta-issue findings
- Triage reports (20260717173346.md, 20260717193243.md, 20260717221319.md) confirm false-positive status
